Sam's Moonlit Memories

September 15, 2023

Date: September 15, 2023

This morning, I awoke to a world subtly changed, a shadow had passed over it. An old friend that I have known since childhood passed away. It hit me like a punch from a giant. It’s a strange feeling that sits in your stomach, heavy, unwelcome. We were close. The kind of close that stitches pieces of your lives together. Once upon a time… A piece of my past, a part of my story, a hole in the patchwork that can’t be mended.

It’s a helpless sort of sadness. I’ve been trying to wrap my head around it, to find some sort of sense or narrative in the irony of fates. But the heart doesn’t always listen to reason, it remembers the laughter, the adventures, the times we argued and made up. They are not here any more, and it’s like trying to hear a song that’s just finished playing.

I’m finding it hard to hold on to the normal, the now. I’m hoping that pouring my thoughts and feelings into this journal will provide a kind of magic that’ll help to put things in order. At least for me. It’s a way of holding onto the memories, of painting them in words so they are less likely to fade into the background. I know they’d want me to remember the good days, and to keep on keeping on, in a world that’s a little darker for their leaving.

September 21, 2023

Date: September 21, 2023

I went to Alex’s funeral. There I stood, amidst the mourning shadows, my heart heavy with the weight of a grief that clung like ivy. The sadness was palpable, filling every corner of the room. The service was touching, and hearing everyone’s kind words and memories eased my pain somewhat.

After the service, I was graciously invited to my friend’s parent’s house. It was like stepping into our shared past, full of laughter and fun. Never I thought I’d be saying goodbye so soon. There I sat for coffee with their family. Our conversation meandered through a landscape of happier times. A nostalgic stroll down the memory lane, each anecdote a cherished echo of our past.

As the sun started to set, I had to go home. Before I went, Alex's parents presented me with a collection of some of Alex’s cherished possessions. Among them, I found a stack of old video games, their cases worn from the countless hours we had lost ourselves in. They also handed me their laptop, and entrusted me with is contents, saying I was the one who probably could make the best use of it. They asked for a simple exchange: any photographs of Alex that I might uncover within its depth, they wished to have.

Clutching these pieces of a life interrupted, I was swept into a tide of bittersweet gratitude. These artefacts, now my charge, were more than mere objects, they were the keys to the treasury of our shared past. I don’t have the mental energy to turn it on today though.

September 25, 2023

Date: September 25, 2023 (edited: January 16, 2024)

Today, I took a deep breath and finally summoned the courage to turn on Alex’s laptop. As the screen hummed to life, it was as if I had entered a minimalist version of their existence. The desktop was eerily clean, only a couple of folders and icons were visible, a stark contrast to the vibrant chaos that was my friend. It felt like a ghostly echo of their life, curated and controlled.

My first task was to fulfil the promise I had made to their parents. I navigated to their image folder, expecting to be hit by a wave of memories consisting of selfies, random images downloaded from the internet, or memes that we shared to each others. But no, it was a sparse collection. Only a handful of photos, each one a carefully snapshot of their life. I saved these onto a usb stick. A small but I'm sure invaluable collection to share with their family.

One of the folder on the desktop was simply called ‘scripts’. It was filled with the scripts they wrote for their Youtube channel. I clicked a few of them of which I recognised the titles. I read for a bit, their voice resonating through each word. I could hear their passion, the enthusiasm that would never again fill a room.

I also found something a bit more unexpected, a folder titled ‘ai’. It contained a series of abstract drawing (painting?). They were not the vibrant, chaotic images I was expecting. Instead, these were darker, almost melancholic. The shapes were heavy, the colors muted. Here and there, the shapes would stumble each others to make something appear: a city, a warehouse, a landfill?

The curated emptiness of their laptop was unsettling. It was as if they had left behind only what they wanted the world to remember, a controlled narrative. It was an echo of him, but also a reminder of the depths that often lay hidden beneath the surface. Tomorrow, I'll share the photos with their family. Tonight, I sit with the heavy silence, their words echoing in my mind, and the haunting images of their artworks.

October 2, 2023

Date: October 2, 2023

Today, I braced myself and opened the laptop once more. The quiet hum of the machine felt like a heartbeat, a lifeline to the world Alex left behind. I navigated back to the 'scripts' folder. The documents stared back at me, frozen in digital time - their thoughts, their words, their voice. They were like vessels, carrying fragments of their spirit within the intricately woven sentences.

I opened one that was last modified the day before their passing - an unfinished script titled "Scrap Metal: The Lost Sound of the 90s". Intrigued, I began to read and soon found myself immersed in an internet mystery. Alex had been investigating a genre from the 90s called "Scrap Metal". Their writing was filled with palpable curiosity, the excitement of a treasure hunt that they were known to love.

Their script was a testament to the power of a community pulling together, piecing together fragmented information and half-forgotten memories. The shared enthusiasm and collective knowledge of an online community, dedicated to uncovering the lost sound of Scrap Metal, was palpable in their words.

The script was raw, sketchy, full of ideas and tangents yet to be explored. The leads they were yet to follow, the songs they were yet to discover. It was like a puzzle with missing pieces, a melody longing for its crescendo.

As the last line of the unfinished script faded into the digital ether, it felt like an unfinished melody, hanging in the air with a note of anticipation. A story left half-told. A journey cut short. Alex's passion for the subject was evident, and it felt almost unjust to leave this script unfinished, this mystery unsolved.

In the quiet of the night, I made a decision. I would pick up where they left off. I would finish the script. For Alex, for their community, for the forgotten sounds of Scrap Metal. I would follow the breadcrumbs they had left behind, diving into this internet mystery, and write the ending that they had envisioned. Maybe it was a way to keep their voice alive, to make sure their echo resonated a little longer. Tonight, I sit with the weight of responsibility, their words resonating in my mind, and the unfinished script that waits for closure.

October 11, 2023

Date: October 11, 2023

Today marks my first foray into the online communities where Alex was a part of the hunt for the elusive Scrap Metal genre. It was a journey into the unknown, a dive into the digital underworld that they were so deeply entrenched in.

My first stop was the subreddit mentioned in the script. To my surprise, it was a private subreddit, a secluded corner of the internet hidden away from prying eyes. I realized I had access to it because I was logged into Alex's account on their computer. I was, in a sense, stepping into their digital shoes. The subreddit was a labyrinth of discussions, ideas, and theories, much like the script itself. There was a wealth of information to sift through. But what struck me was the sense of community. This wasn't just a place for sharing information; it was a gathering place for a tightly-knit group of enthusiasts, bound together by their shared facination for Scrap Metal.

Next, I ventured into their Discord. This, too, was a private server, a digital sanctuary for the Scrap Metal hunters, to which I had access since Alex was still logged in. (I set the setting to be invisible in Discord, I didn't want anyone to freak out my seeing Alex connected.) Here, their interactions were more informal, more personal. They had developed complex relationships, created their own private jokes, and formed traditions over the years. It was a window into a world that felt both strange and familiar, like stepping into a party where you didn't know anyone, but everyone knew you.

This dive into the online communities was a treasure trove of information, but it wasn't going to be easy to navigate. The friendly discussions, while endearing, made it difficult to separate the wheat from the chaff. The task of unraveling the mystery of Scrap Metal seemed even more daunting now. But it was clear that this was a necessary step. I was walking the path that Alex had tread, trying to see the world through their eyes. And this world was far more intricate, more vibrant than I could have ever imagined.

I can't help but feel a mix of apprehension and excitement. There's so much to learn, so much to uncover. This is just the beginning.
